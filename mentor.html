A BRIEF BIOGRAPHY OF MY MENTOR

MARGARET HAMILTON

Half a century ago, MIT played a critical role in the development of the flight software for NASA's
Apollo program, which landed humans on the Moon for the first time in 1969. One of the many contributors,
to this effort was Margaret Hamilton, a computer scientist who led the Software Engineering Division of
the MIT Instrumentation Laboratory, which in 1961 contracted with NASA to develop the Apollo program,s guidance
system. For her work during this period, Hamilton has been credited with popularizing the concept of software engineering.
